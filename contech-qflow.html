The Problem, the Opportunity and Vision

Qualis Flow is a SaaS platform using AI to manage environmental risk on construction projects. It pulls live data from sensors so teams can track, predict, and analyse issues in real time—staying compliant, avoiding fines, and protecting the environment. Built for environmental consultants and project managers, it simplifies compliance on large-scale projects.

Consultants previously spent days manually collecting air quality data, only to analyse it after the fact. This delay meant teams were reacting to breaches instead of preventing them.

Qflow's Air Quality module automates data aggregation and uses AI trained on historical readings to predict pollution based on construction schedules. Project managers can adjust plans and see risks instantly, turning compliance into a proactive, strategic tool.

With an early client ready to test on a high-risk project and limited competition in this niche, the focus was building a scalable, real-time solution for multiple clients.

Turning Sensor Chaos into Clear Actions

Additional key findings included:

  • Consultants received email alerts with time, location, and exceedance data — but still needed to understand pollutant behaviour and identify causes without hours of manual data downloads.
  • Wanted quick visual insights for stakeholder meetings, with accurate underlying data always within reach.
  • Needed to compare multiple gases, sensors, and data sources freely, in a tool that felt easy and trustworthy.
  • Had to scale across complex projects — many sensors, gases, and manufacturers, with flexible thresholds for different regulations and user needs.
  • Needed predictive monitoring to catch trends early, not just react to exceedance events.

MVP Focus

Building an MVP meant focusing on what mattered most:

  • Fast, responsive access to sensor APIs
  • Smart caching and quick loading
  • GPS-enabled mapping to pinpoint issues

All while managing tight deadlines, shifting terminology, and limited domain knowledge.

Scope & Tiered Implementation

Scope creep led to tiered implementation — skimmed, semi-skimmed, and full-fat — to ensure delivery, delaying some features until post-launch. By designing complete user flows and edge cases upfront, I aimed to future-proof the product beyond the MVP.

Introducing animation as a quick walkthrough technique to grab the user's attention when initially opening up air quality.

Hover function idea reveals monitor/sensors and their data lines. Suggested by the CTO after a show-and-tell, the hover feature was designed to distinguish multiple data lines without confusing accurate data viewing with Gantt chart filtering.

Programme Gantt Chart Feature

We tested how construction data, like work packages and traffic, affects pollutant behavior. The Gantt chart idea was technically complex, so I suggested a simpler workaround: manual uploads instead of relying on Qflow automation. Not ideal, but engineering built a quick Excel upload hack. We took a "let's try it" approach and tracked usage in Mixpanel to decide if it was worth pursuing further.

Updating the Exceedance Feature. Clients wanted a search for sensors, but since their main goal was finding exceedances above specific monitors, I brainstormed with the lead developer and came up with a feasible alternative — a simple checkbox to highlight them.

Candid Reflections, Competitors & What I've Learned

Hover over the video to watch a 2-minute snippet on Power BI trends impacting Qflow business direction.

Throughout the project — and increasingly toward the end — I noticed an emerging trend within the research: specialists were funding their own Power BI training, making it a formidable direct competitor within potential clients' existing Microsoft environments. This pattern accelerated across sectors like waste collection and material delivery, where robust data visualisation proved too valuable to ignore.

The pilot revealed tough market truths. The client didn't convert after the trial — they could hire a junior analyst with BI training for less than Qflow's subscription. Without the promised advanced AI forecasting feature integrated with construction work schedules and the exceedance threshold line still undeveloped (scheduled for the next release), the company couldn't justify a resource-heavy push based on a single client's commitment.

When COVID-19 hit, Qflow quickly pivoted to focus on AI and ML for more attainable and successful modules already in the field. Integrating Power BI API to work with clients' existing tools, rather than competing against them.

A snippet of the actual in-product graph visualisation before more UI styling refinement, used during the client's 3-month pilot.

Not everything was lost — the app provided valuable usage insights that became great training material. As a designer, I learned how good research can save a company time and money, showing that without the resources to build something truly different, you risk creating a product no one wants — like offering a skateboard when they're after a car. 🛹 🚲 🛵 🚙

Design System & Other Modules

Air Quality required unique UI components to address user needs, whereas other products — such as Waste and Materials — followed a more CRUD-based (create, read, update, and delete) approach, utilising Material Design principles. Collaborating with the product owner & engineers, we scaled these modules beyond MVP toward an enterprise-level system.

I created Qflow's first unified design system with consistent patterns, visual language, and reusable components. Integrated with Storybook, it documented UI behaviour, animation, illustration, branding, and accessibility — helping engineers standardise capabilities, speed up implementation, and support skill expansion for those seeking full-stack experience.
