# Qflow Materials & Waste Risk Notifications — Audio Narration Script

---

Hey, thanks for listening. This is the Qualis Flow notifications case study — a design challenge centred on giving environmental managers real-time visibility across construction sites, and building a navigation system to match.

---

## What is Qflow?

Qualis Flow is an environmental management platform that holistically tracks, monitors, and predicts construction projects' environmental risk. One of its core propositions was giving environment managers the ability to stay on top of any triggered alert — as close to real time as possible — across all the projects they oversaw.

Any data from scanned tickets or API integration needed to notify users of government compliance issues, helping managers report, investigate, and rectify quickly. The reasons behind this were clear: reduce accidental installation of non-compliant building materials that could be forcibly ripped out later; avoid financial penalties; save time and be ready for government audits; avoid delays or closures; and finish a project with a clean bill of health so companies could win more contracts.

Qflow was already trialling their MVP on two sites — collecting data by photographing paper tickets and notifying customers of anomalies. My aim was to improve the web app's UX and UI while adding new features, and to think ahead about how artificial intelligence would automate and enhance data-informed decisions.

---

## My Role and What I Designed

After discovery sessions with clients and experiencing their landscape firsthand, I moved on to researching competitors — for example Smart Waste. I produced various flows, either independently or collaboratively with the product owner and CTO, to map out multiple paths, restrictions, and solutions for the stage 2 MVP.

Because notifications would drive the platform and navigation, I mapped out how different user levels would interact with various alerts. I eventually reorganised these insights into Qflow's homepage — aimed at satisfying a range of target users. The main concept resembles a Facebook or Twitter-style feed: a list of alerts, always on the homepage, always visible.

I introduced a flashing alert button as part of the visual language of the platform — always used to tell users something needs attention, or to guide their next step. I also started testing illustrations as visual anchors to reinforce missing information, and to develop a Qflow brand character that mirrored the founders' sense of humour. Further development could have meant animation to congratulate users when they'd resolved an issue — giving the platform a more delightful presence.

At this point, Qflow was also looking into the scalability of the software for potential global release. This meant investigating information architecture, localisation of microcopy, multiple modules, organisations, settings, and homepage — all within one cohesive navigation.

---

## Navigation for Enterprise

I researched the landscape of relevant roles within construction using secondary sources — advertised job vacancies, conference snippets, and subject matter stakeholders. This revealed a pyramid structure of different user levels, their crossover between projects, and their overall responsibilities.

I wanted to avoid breadcrumb navigation, which could create additional confusion, while still giving users the freedom to move in and out of different levels. The concept prototype I developed shows the functional flow of a user navigating between the homepage, content pages, project and organisation pages, and settings.

For top-level users — management and directors overseeing multiple construction projects — I designed a grouping feature that let them aggregate data and see the overall picture through visualisation.

---

## Results

The work produced a scalable design framework pattern that could be reused across all Qflow products, increased engagement with customers, and helped the founders envision tangible solutions to the complex organisational and permission structures of construction sites.
